Hungarian Innovation Ecosystem Map

New intuitive and more user-friendly UI for NIÜ (Hungarian National Innovation Agency) on the Hungarian Innovation Ecosystem Map.

About the Project

The Hungarian National Innovation Agency already had a Hungarian Innovation Ecosystem Map with a very simple and early-stage user interface. They had a call for tenders to redesign the site and make it more intuitive and interactive to make it easier and more fun to use. We applied as a team with Hajnal Mészáros, Luca Sára Kiss and myself and worked on the project together.

The Problem

When we started to investigate the old design of the map, we identified many pain points where the use and navigation of the map was confusing for users. In order to understand how to develop a new concept, we collected the patterns that did not work well and that we wanted to avoid. These patterns were the following:

  • Identical appearance of clickable and non-clickable elements.
  • Unclear hierarchy and grouping of information.
  • Information noise, difficult to find the relevant information and the connection between them.
  • Intrusive popups lacking meaningful information.
“Our goal was to eliminate popups and present all essential information directly on the screen, using engaging yet unobtrusive interactions and animations to ensure a seamless and enjoyable map experience.”

Research

To support my hypotheses and gather data, I used both primary and secondary research methods to explore the challenges faced by rural women caring for elderly relatives. A key finding was that they do not see themselves as carers but rather view these tasks as a duty. This research led to identifying four key pain points, offering deeper insights and potential solutions.

These pain points were the following:

Feeling alone
  • Isolation is a big problem in rural area.
  • There is no one to talk to about frustration.
  • Sensitive topics.
Feeling unqualified
  • These people have no proper training in nursing care.
Feeling stressed
  • Managing someone else's life.
  • Knowing that it's the last chapter in your loved one's life.
  • Dealing with early grief.
Feeling disorganised
  • Doctor's visit and medication.
  • Constant need for planning and administration.

Although there are various software solutions for task management and optimization, as well as resources for self-care and mental well-being, there is no digital product specifically designed to support home caregivers for the elderly and address their unique needs.
