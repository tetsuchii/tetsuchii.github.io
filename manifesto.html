Design Manifesto

Introduction

I'm absolutely not a fan of manifestos. They often come across as rigid, dogmatic, and disconnected from real-world practice. However, if I really have to do it, and this is the case right now, I think it would look like this:

My 10 points

1. I design to make complex systems understandable.

2. I create clarity through structure, intention, and reduction.

3. I treat technology as a medium for learning.

4. I design experiences that are human, and responsible.

5. I prototype early to learn, not to prove.

6. I design beyond screens.

7. I value transparency, accessibility, and long-term usefulness.

8. I teach by simplifying without dumbing down.

9. I design with curiosity, evidence, and empathy.

10. I build experiences that help people feel more capable.
