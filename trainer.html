trAIner

trAIner is a yoga practice application developed as my BSc thesis, enabling users to practice yoga at home with guidance from an AI instructor.

About the Project

There are so many incredible third-party services that can enhance applications, and with the power of machine learning, we’re now able to solve problems that were once impossible. When I worked on this project (February 2022 - December 2022), technologies like ChatGPT hadn’t yet emerged, and image generation was still in its early stages. This highlights just how rapidly this field is evolving. If such advancements were possible back then, imagine what we could achieve today. With a bit more development, this application could become the next "Duolingo" for workouts and yoga, offering a safe, accessible, and free way for people to work out from home, on their own time.

The Problem

Many people prefer working out at home for various reasons, but a common challenge—especially with yoga—is the lack of a professional supervisor to provide guidance, improve technique, and help prevent injuries.

How might we create an application that provides users with personalized, safe, and effective yoga practice by leveraging AI supervision to ensure correct form and reduce the risk of injury?

Considerations

During the development, I took into account various factors to ensure the application would be accessible and usable by a wide range of people worldwide. My test device ran the minimum Android version required for Google ML Kit, so I made sure the application would work seamlessly across all currently used Android versions, and even those lower than the most recent ones. Today, less than 3% of Android users globally are still on Android 7 or earlier, yet my application runs smoothly on Android 5, which demonstrates its broad compatibility and reliability.

Source


To enhance safety, users are required to log in to save their personal settings, such as whether they are currently injured or pregnant. This information helps tailor the yoga experience to their specific needs, as certain poses may not be recommended or feasible for them due to their condition. While this isn't a perfect solution, it reflects a thoughtful approach to user safety and demonstrates the effort to create a positive and secure user experience. Once the settings are saved, and the user returns to the list of yoga poses, only the poses that are considered safe for their condition will be available.


User flow

It is in the rules, that the robot has to fit into a 10x10 cm big box, but the height is not limited. So, I tried to make it taller, but still put the center of gravity as low as possible, for stability. On the front a oblique shield helps, to disorient and dislodge the enemy.

Final Deliverables

The final prototype of the robot is shown below. It was cut out of PVC sheets and fixed with screws. The angle of the distance sensors can be changed by inserting a screwdriver into the front centre hole of the robot. (This is useful if the opposing robot is shorter).

Take away

This project was my diploma thesis for a Computer Engineering BSc. Through this experience, I realized how much I would prefer to focus more on planning and design rather than development. Due to time constraints and the focus on functionality, there wasn't much opportunity to create a polished and user-friendly UI. The primary challenge was addressing the issue of supervision, which ultimately was solved, but the available functions were already stretched to their limits.
